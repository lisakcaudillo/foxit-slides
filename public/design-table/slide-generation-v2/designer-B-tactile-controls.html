Round 2 · Designer B · Tactile / Physical Controls — "The Composer's Desk"

Original vs Round 1: Round 1 went neutral — identical quiet glass pills, one violet accent, the generic SaaS form. Clutter just turned grey. This goes the other way: each control gets a distinct physical form so you read its job by feel, not by reading a label. Material concept: every object is milled from one machined-panel system — same top light-line, bottom inner shadow, drop shadow, and press physics — sitting on a frosted desk over the Digital-Wallet glassmorphism orbs, so colour stays rich but unified. The toggle is a real hardware rocker: a bevelled well with a travelling thumb that springs right and lights violet when ON — state reads by position + label + check, never colour alone. Accessibility: ≥4.5:1 text, ≥44px hit areas, visible focus rings, role="switch", and prefers-reduced-motion snaps every state with no travel.

Press-depth on the key, spring on the rocker, lift on the chips — every object shares one material.
Dial (slide count): a recessed well + raised thumbwheels + a darkened mechanical counter window. The −/+ sink on press; the number reads like an odometer. Not a flat pill — you can feel "increment" before you read it.
Rocker (auto-images): a bevelled well with a chunky travelling thumb. OFF seats left & recessed-slate; ON springs right, lights violet, swaps the label to "On" + check. Position + label carry the state, not colour.
Paint chips (theme): physical samples seated in a tray. Active chip lifts out (−3px) with a violet ring like you've picked it up. Reads as choosing a material, not tapping a rainbow badge.
One material system: every object shares the same top light-line, inner-shadow well, drop shadow, and press travel. Distinct forms, one toolkit — that's how clutter resolves without going neutral.
