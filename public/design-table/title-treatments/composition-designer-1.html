Designer 1 — Dynamic Image + Title Composition

The title slide is one editorial title kit — eyebrow + hairline rule + headline (with one italic accent word) + byline — that re-anchors and re-fits to whatever space the deck engine's image leaves. The image arrives in different forms (a half, a diagonal cut, a band, or full-bleed); the title doesn't fight it, it moves to the open region and adopts the contrast treatment that region needs (column-fit beside, top/bottom-anchored above/below, scrim-backed on top of). One family, many real compositions — with a type-only baseline that never breaks.

1Vertical half — image one side, title column the other (+ mirror)
2Diagonal split — clip-path cut, title in the complementary triangle (+ mirror)
3Band — image TOP, title anchored BOTTOM
4Band — image BOTTOM, title anchored TOP
5Full-bleed image + theme scrim, title overlaid
6Type-only fallback — no image, motif + fonts carry it
