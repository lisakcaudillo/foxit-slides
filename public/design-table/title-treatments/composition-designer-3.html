Dynamic Composition Spec Library

Round 1 shipped type-only covers and Lisa rejected them: the title slide is dynamic — the deck engine generates a real image, drops it in one of several forms, and the title treatment must adapt so image and title co-exist. This is the buildable spec library for that adaptation. Six composition layouts, each rendered on a real theme over a real generated image, each specified with exact geometry (split ratio / clip-path polygon / band height), the title bounding box, the scrim rule and its driving Theme token, a token-to-anatomy map, WCAG AA contrast, and a reconcile line back to cover-tiers.ts's imageRole. An engineer implements directly from each card.

Mirror

Title anchor & treatment

Anchored to the wide corner of its triangle (top-left here). Full editorial stack. The italic accent lands on the last headline word so it never crosses the diagonal.

Scrim

Seam only. A 2px line in linkColor/titleColor on the diagonal sells the cut. No text overlaps the image, so no scrim. If a headline word must cross into the image triangle, apply the Full-Bleed scrim (§05) locally behind that word.

05

Full-Bleed Overlay

Image fills the whole slide; a theme-driven scrim guarantees contrast; the title overlays. The most cinematic — and the one that requires the scrim contract.

Where the

Volt · Company Kickoff
A
scrim 0 → .86 (180deg), text zone only

Theme: Volt (creative / dark / cinematic)  ·  Image: cinematic  ·  A bottom-anchored title over scrim

Geometry

Image
Full bleed: inset:0; object-fit:cover.
Title bounds
Bottom-anchored: left:7% · bottom:9% · width:78%. (Top-anchored variant: top:11% with the scrim flipped to 0deg.)
Anchor choice
Pick the darker / quieter third of the image for the title (sample image luminance per third; engine already does this for image-aware bounds).

Title anchor & treatment

Editorial stack anchored to top OR bottom — never centered over a busy middle. Headline shifts toward white/light fill so it reads on the scrim regardless of theme titleColor.

Scrim — the contract

Color source: derive from the theme's dark anchor — primaryBg for dark themes (here #131D2E), else a neutral ink #0B1F3A.
Direction: 180deg if title is bottom-anchored, 0deg if top.
Opacity ramp (text end): 0.55 → 0.86. Clear end: 0.
Brightness rule: measure mean luminance of the title third. L > 0.6 (bright image) → bump text-end to 0.90; L < 0.3 (already dark) → ease to 0.45. Scrim covers only the third the text occupies, never the full image.
Text: force light fill (#FFFFFF / theme light stop) when over a scrim, ignoring solid titleColor.

Geometry

Title bounds
left:8% · width:74%, full height, vertically centered. Headline is the largest of any layout (~50px at 405h) since it owns the slide.
Motif
<CoverArt theme dark={tone==='dark'} /> in the theme's intentional corner (compass → bottom-right). Opacity 12–22% (16% here).
Pattern
Render pagePattern at full pagePatternSize across the slide.

Title anchor & treatment

Full editorial stack, vertically centered, given room to be oversized. The motif sits behind, never competing with the headline column.

Scrim

None. No image. The motif's own low opacity (12–22%) is the only veiling. Never raise it past 22% or it competes with type.
