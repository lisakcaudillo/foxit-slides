Designer 1 — Editorial Title Treatments

A title slide should read like the title page of a hardcover book — never a body slide with bigger text. Each treatment below combines moves no body slide ever wears (an eyebrow with a hairline rule, a headline with one italic or accent word, a bottom byline strip that frames the page as a cover) and promotes the theme's own CoverArt motif from background decoration to ambient texture at 12–22% opacity. The eight treatments are typographically distinct from one another, and each is rendered on a different real Compose theme so the variety reads at a glance — the system is a family, not one template recolored eight times. Every headline and label clears WCAG AA on its theme.
