Design Table · Designer 1

Geometric & structured title decoration

Six vector motifs that extend the existing CoverArt vocabulary — subtle, theme-tinted SVG built to sit behind a cover title without fighting legibility. Every motif renders at 6–16% opacity, anchors to a corner or edge so the center stays clear for the headline, and carries pointer-events: none. Each tints to the active theme; nothing is hardcoded to one palette.

Sample content is identical on every card so options compare directly. Decoration is theme-tint only — independent of the photo-image toggle.

How these stay subtle & theme-adaptive. Every motif draws with 1–2 theme palette colors (here violet #C8A8FF / pink #FFB4D4 on dark; purple #6B3FA0 / amber #C0561E on light) at 6–16% effective opacity, with a radial or linear mask pulling the decoration away from the title's optical center toward a corner or edge — so the headline always clears WCAG AA (≥4.5:1). Swapping the two stroke colors is the only change needed to retheme any motif; nothing is hardcoded. All layers are pointer-events: none and sit at z-index 1 behind the content at z-index 2.

Implementation note. Each becomes a new case in CoverArt.tsx (or a small GeometricDecor map keyed by motif id) — same viewBox + preserveAspectRatio slice contract already used there, with ink/accent driven by the theme instead of literal hexes.
