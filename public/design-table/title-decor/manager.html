The Curated Title-Decor Pool

Ten subtle, vector, theme-adaptive decorations drawn faithfully from the designer showcases the PM already reviewed — all six of Designer 2's organic family, three editorial motifs from Designer 3 (gradient dots, single-line arc, hairline frame), plus one Manager-suggested addition (the refined thin hexagon that echoes the reference cover shape). Every motif is pure SVG/CSS, tinted through 1–2 palette tokens at 6–18% opacity, sits behind the title, is independent of the image toggle, and never fights legibility. The title is the hero on every card.

Recommendation

Ship all 10 as the v1 pool. The set is deliberately two complementary registers: the six organic motifs carry mood and warmth (the refined successor to the "light rays" the PM loves), while the four editorial motifs bring quiet structure and premium-print restraint. A deck rotating through both registers reads as a designed system, not a single repeated flourish.

  • Default rotation pool (all 10): the planner picks one per title slide, weighted by the deck's theme archetype — organic for warm/cinematic themes, editorial for slate/paper themes — so the motif always matches the mood.
  • Re-tint, never re-author: each motif reads --tint / --tint2 / --neutral from the active theme (the CoverArt.tsx pattern). The "Variety in action" strip proves one engine produces five distinct covers without a single hardcoded hue.
  • If we must trim to a tighter v1: ship 6 — Aurora Gradient Mesh, Flowing Ribbons, Topographic Contours, Halftone Dot Gradient, Single-Line Arc, Refined Thin Hexagon. That keeps three organic + three editorial and covers every mood with zero redundancy.
