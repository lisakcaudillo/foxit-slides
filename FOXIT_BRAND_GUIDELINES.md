## 1. BRAND STRATEGY & VOICE

### 1.1 Mission
Foxit develops innovative PDF products and services that empower customers to be more productive and efficient with digital documents. The tagline "Do More with Documents" is a core brand promise delivered with every product.

### 1.2 Vision
Foxit aspires to be the global leader in PDF solutions — empowering businesses and individuals with fast, efficient, and intuitive document management tools.

### 1.3 Key Messaging Pillars
All communications must align with these three pillars:
- **Efficiency** — Empowering smarter work; eliminating friction in document workflows
- **Security** — Trust at every touchpoint; enterprise-grade safeguards at every step
- **Innovation** — Redefining document experiences with AI-driven tools and future-ready features

### 1.4 Brand Voice
- **Clear and Concise:** Simplify complex ideas. Prioritize customer benefits over technical jargon.
- **Supportive and Encouraging:** Speak as a trusted partner empowering users to achieve more.
- **Authoritative but Approachable:** Demonstrate expertise with confidence while maintaining a conversational, inviting tone.
- **Engaging and Conversational:** Foster relationships and dialogue across all channels.

### 1.5 Tone Guidelines
- **Professional, yet personable** — accessible and human
- **Confident, not condescending** — knowledgeable but humble
- **Clear, not overly technical** — avoid jargon; show how solutions improve workflows
- **Educational, not overly salesy** — encourage action without pressure; customer-first

## 2. LOGO

### 2.1 Overview
The Foxit logo is the cornerstone of the visual identity. The fox-tail-inspired "X" provides visual clarity and strengthens recognition at all sizes. The brand strategy makes "Fox" and fox imagery synonymous with the world of PDFs.

### 2.2 Approved Logo Versions
- **Wordmark** — The primary representation; default for all uses.
- **Icon** (the "X" / fox tail mark) — Reserved for favicon use or contexts where the broader brand is already established.

### 2.3 Permitted Colors
RULE: Only **orange** and **white** versions of the logo are permitted.
- Primary orange logo → use on **light backgrounds** for maximum visibility.
- White logo → use on dark or colored backgrounds per palette.

### 2.4 Retired Logos
RULE: Never use any retired logos including the old main logo or the PhantomPDF logo. They no longer represent the updated branding.

### 2.5 Minimum Size
- **Print:** No smaller than 0.25" / 6.35mm
- **Digital/Web:** No smaller than 18px

### 2.6 Clear Space
The Foxit "X" is the foundational unit of measurement. All spacing is proportional to the X's width and height:
- **The 'X' is the anchor:** every element is sized as a ratio or multiple of the X dimensions.
- **Proportional harmony:** eliminates fixed measurements (px/pt) — brand scales fluidly across all applications.

### 2.7 Logo Placement Rules
| Rule | Status |
|---|---|
| Left-aligned | ✅ **Standard** — always preferred |
| Center-aligned | ⚠️ **Exception** — only for short headlines, pull quotes, symmetrical layouts, or standalone elements |
| Right-aligned | ❌ **Prohibited** |
| Bottom placement | ❌ **Prohibited** (diminishes prominence). Exception: organic social-media posts for Foxit X may place logo at bottom. |

### 2.8 Tagline Lockup
- The wordmark may lock up with the tagline: **"Do More with Documents"**
- When using the tagline lockup, do not use the logo smaller than 1/4 of the "X" tall.

### 2.9 Co-Branding Lockups
- Place co-branded logos next to or under the Foxit wordmark with a simple rule divider.
- For partner **wordmarks**: match partner letter height to the "X" height in Foxit logo.
- For partner **icon-based logos**: match overall height to the "F" height in Foxit logo.
- Vertical lockups: Foxit logo on top, partner below, dividing rule between, partner center-aligned to Foxit logo width.

### 2.10 Logo Misuse — DO NOT:
- ❌ Add a drop shadow
- ❌ Add a stroke/outline
- ❌ Rotate the wordmark or icon
- ❌ Stretch horizontally or vertically
- ❌ Modify logo colors
- ❌ Place on colors outside the brand palette
- ❌ Use illegible color combinations over photography

### 2.11 Trademark
The Foxit logo is a registered trademark of Foxit Software, Inc. The ™ symbol is not displayed in standard use, but the logo is protected under trademark law. Unauthorized use, reproduction, or alteration is prohibited.

### 3.2 Color Usage Hierarchy
- **Foxit Orange** — Present in **90%** of designs (prominent or subtle). Cornerstone of brand recognition.
- **Shadowberry & Eclipse** — Create contrast and depth; balance the boldness of orange.
- **Golden** — Used sparingly at **~10%**; adds energy without compromising legibility.

### 3.3 Color Rules
- RULE: Foxit Orange must NOT be used as a full-page background for documents, web pages, or large hero sections (too intense; harms readability). Exception: smaller compositions like organic social posts, or event booths/large-scale structures.
- RULE: Golden must NEVER be used as a primary color or background. It must always be paired with Eclipse or Shadowberry.
- RULE: Do not use logo on colors outside the brand palette.
- RULE: Do not use illegible color combinations.
- RULE: Do not modify logo colors.

## 4. TYPOGRAPHY

### 4.1 Font System
| Purpose | Font | Weights |
|---|---|---|
| **Headlines / Display** | **Barlow** | Regular, Medium, SemiBold, Bold, ExtraBold, Black |
| **Body / Communications** | **Open Sans** | Light, Regular, SemiBold, Bold, ExtraBold |
| **Web-safe Fallback** | **Tahoma** | Regular, Bold |

### 4.2 Barlow Rules
- USE FOR: Headlines, display text, special typographic elements, large-scale messages.
- DO NOT USE FOR: Large runs of body copy, small text, legal disclaimers.
- ❌ **Barlow Condensed** — PROHIBITED (reduced legibility).
- ❌ **Barlow Semi Condensed** — Not recommended for branding.
- ✅ DO use varying font weights to emphasize key words.

### 4.3 Open Sans Rules
- USE FOR: Body copy, presentations, annotations, footnotes, digital communications.
- ❌ **Open Sans Condensed** — NOT PERMITTED (reduces readability).
- Always select Open Sans for web typography when available.

### 4.4 Typography Hierarchy Rules
- ❌ **ALL CAPS is prohibited** — reduces readability, conveys unintended aggression.
  - Exception: Eyebrows only, limited to **3 words maximum**.
- ✅ **Headlines** must always be **Title Case**. Can vary font weight.
- ✅ **Eyebrows** — 3 words max, styled in all caps.
- ✅ **CTAs** — 3 words max, **Open Sans SemiBold (600 weight)**.

### 4.5 Line Spacing
| Element | Line Spacing |
|---|---|
| Headlines & Titles | 110%–120% of font size |
| Body Text | 120%–145% of font size |

Reference conversions: 1 inch = 96px, 1 point = 1.333px.

### 4.6 Text Alignment
| Alignment | Status |
|---|---|
| **Left-aligned** | ✅ Preferred — follows natural reading pattern |
| **Center-aligned** | ⚠️ Acceptable for balanced/symmetrical designs; must remain readable |
| **Right-aligned** | ❌ NOT PERMITTED |
| **Full-justified** | ❌ PROHIBITED — disrupts natural spacing and legibility |

## 5. PHOTOGRAPHY

### 5.1 Guiding Principles
All photography must express these three principles:
- **Relatability** — Moments that feel familiar and achievable; audience sees themselves in the narrative.
- **Connection** — Show how PDFs bring people together; teamwork, collaboration, problem-solving.
- **Candid** — Authentic and approachable; avoid overly polished/staged settings; natural environments.

### 5.2 General Rules
- Select images with ample **white space** for overlaid typography.
- Every photograph must align with accompanying written content.
- Emphasize **clarity and professionalism**; avoid cluttered/overly staged visuals.
- Incorporate **devices** (phones, tablets, laptops, desktops, screens) to showcase enabling technology.

### 5.3 Portrait Guidelines
- Editorial-style, lifestyle approach — minimal styling, natural contexts, candid feel.
- Clean, uncluttered backgrounds with shallow depth of field.
- Bright, natural lighting — no artificial studio setups.
- Allow ample space for headlines or graphic overlays.

### 5.4 Photography Checklist
1. Show natural movement — candid, fluid, relaxed poses
2. Reflect global reach — diverse community (ages, abilities, body types, genders, races, ethnicities, industries, nationalities)
3. Clean environments — simple, uncluttered
4. Natural lighting — soft shadows, warm tones; no harsh lighting
5. No external branding — no other logos unless explicitly approved (partnerships/integrations)
6. Simple compositions — clear focal point, ample negative space, easily croppable
7. Authenticity — no lens flares, flashes, or harsh studio lighting
8. Highlight connection — include devices representing collaboration tools

### 5.5 Photography Do's and Don'ts
| ✅ DO | ❌ DON'T |
|---|---|
| High saturation, warm/rich grading | Low saturation, cold/washed grading |
| Natural, candid, authentic shots | Cliche, over-staged, unnaturally-posed |
| Modern-day attire and environments | Outdated fashion, environments |
| Inclusive range of professionals | Flash photography or harshly lit photos |

### 5.6 Photo Containers
- **Tail Container** — Uses the iconic tail from the "X" in the Foxit logo to mask photography. Orientation: bottom-left sweeping to top-right (matching logo form). Subject parts may extend beyond container for dimensional effect. Use against simple, uncluttered backgrounds.
- **Square Container** — Structured alternative; accommodates cut-out or full uncut visuals. Effective for balance and simplicity; works with minimal/less busy backgrounds.

## 6. PRODUCT IMAGERY

### 6.1 Three Representation Types
Choose based on what brings the most clarity:

| Type | Best For | Rules |
|---|---|---|
| **High-fidelity** | Educational demos, feature walkthroughs | Mirror actual deployed product exactly; include menu bars, nav elements; show literal interfaces for integrations; crop closer rather than abstract |
| **Simplified** | Focusing on a specific feature/benefit | Abstracted UIs; enlarged elements get drop shadow or glassmorphic effect for dimensionality; text lines can be represented as rounded rectangles; colors must stay within brand palette |
| **Interfaces in Photography** | Emotional connection, real-world context | Real people on real devices with actual high-fidelity Foxit interfaces on-screen; follow Photography guidelines for the imagery itself |

## 7. VISUAL ELEMENTS

### 7.1 CTA Button Design
| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Shape | Rectangular with subtle rounded corners (5-10px radius) |
| Height/Width | Hug content |
| Padding | Top & Bottom: 16px; Left & Right: 32px |
| Font | Open Sans, SemiBold/600 weight, 18px |
| Primary CTA | Foxit Orange background, white text |
| Secondary CTA | Eclipse background, white text |
| Content | **3 words maximum** |
| ❌ Avoid | Overly rounded "pill-shaped" buttons |

### 7.2 Iconography
- Clean, modern style with optional color accents.
- **Color-accented icons:** One section uses Foxit Orange or Shadowberry; other section is neutral (white or black).
- **Solid color icons:** Entirely in one brand palette color or neutral tone.

### 7.3 Graphic Patterns
- **Waves** — Symbolize movement and connectivity; use as subtle background/accent; never overpower content.
- **Dots** — Reinforce clean/modern aesthetic; use sparingly with varying sizes/densities; do not overwhelm primary messaging.

### 7.4 Fox Mascot
- Business casual look; modern, dynamic, professional.
- Tail echoes the "X" tail in the logo — reinforces visual identity.
- Adds personality and warmth; playful yet sophisticated.

### 7.5 Illustrations
- ❌ **Corporate Memphis-style illustrations are PROHIBITED** — lacks brand differentiation.
- Prioritize Foxit photography and product imagery for visual storytelling.
- If illustration is required, use minimal, UI-inspired vector elements that stay secondary to content and consistent with Foxit's visual system.

### 7.6 Data Visualization
- **Primary colors:** Foxit Orange, Shadowberry, Eclipse, White. Use tints/shades for subgroups.
- **Call-out numbers:** Set in Foxit Orange or Shadowberry at large scale.
- Subtle gradients and drop shadows may add dimensionality, but **never sacrifice legibility**.

### 7.7 Glassmorphism
- Adds dimensionality with subtle geometric shapes and glass-like (frosted/opaque) effects.
- Creates layering where graphics/photography overlap and intersect.
- Use selectively to focus and highlight key elements — do not overwhelm.
- Supports clean, modern aesthetic with lightness and clarity.
